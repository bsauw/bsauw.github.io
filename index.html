    Micromouse

    This robot is designed to navigate a large maze using a combination of artificial intelligence and various sensors mounted all about the D-ring at the front. Two DC motors drive the wheels, and each side has an encoder to determine values such as distance travelled, degrees turned, and rate of change of both.

    A comprehensive report regarding engineering specifications, construction of the chassis, and development of the artificial intelligence is currently being written.

    • Date: January 2015
    • Focus: Design, Manufacturing, Rapid Prototyping, Controls

    Automatic Vegetable Slicer (AVS)

    The AVS was born out of a need to quickly and efficiently cut fruits and vegetables for use in salads or other meals. The mechanism uses an automated sharp blade to cut items fed to it through the guide chamber. The cut items then fall into the removable metal collection chamber underneath. Safety features include an acrylic housing over the blade and an emergency stop button that automatically cuts power to the system.

    A poster including engineering specifications and construction details may be found here.

    • Date: August 2014
    • Focus: Design, Manufacturing

    Visualization of Vortices on a Cone

    This project concerns vortex formation as transitional winds passes over a spinning cone. The apparatus relies on a sting to support the motor and aluminum cone assemblies, all of which are mounted on a rack and pinion setup. This allows for the angle of attack of the cone to be altered so that the effects of changing AoA on vortex formation may be visualized. Additionally, force sensors will be fitted on the cone to determine [academics]. This assembly will be placed in a 80cmx80cm wind tunnel with flow speeds up to 20m/s. Titanium dioxide will be used to visualize both vortex formation and flow over the cone.

    A comprehensive report regarding engineering specifications, construction of the assembly, and visualized results is currently being written.

    • Date: August 2014
    • Focus: Design, Manufacturing, Aerodynamics

    Self-Inverting Pendulum

    This project involves a pendulum that self-inverts itself through the use of energy balance and basic dynamics. The cart that allows for self-inversion uses two encoders to output linear velocity of the cart and angular velocity of the pendulum. These values are then fed to a Simulink program that uses observer-controller feedback to correct the cart's current velocity.

    • Date: March 2014
    • Focus: Controls

    Barbot

    The BarBot allows for efficient drink mixing in a quick and consistent manner. A microcontroller controls self-priming pumps that combine various alcoholic beverages in a mixing chamber before serving the drink. A large advantage of this automation is that the pumps supply the exact ratios required of each ingredient, resulting in perfectly mixed drinks. The microcontroller also houses an extensive library of popular drinks, such as the Cosmopolitan or Vodka Cranberry.

    A flyer detailing the capabilities of the Barbot may be found here.

    • Date: August 2013
    • Focus: Mechatronics, Design, Manufacturing

    Anti-Sway Controls

    This cart traverses along a track while preventing its pendulum from swaying. This technique, better known as anti-sway, is implemented on cranes in order to prevent payloads from oscillating or vibrating. The combination of input shaping and robust PID allows the cart to make adjustments to its velocity to keep the pendulum steady even when accelerating or braking. Encoders mounted to the pendulum and motors allow for quick data collection and comparisons between zero-vibration and zero-vibration derivative methods of input shaping.

    A report regarding the development and production of the cart as well as the controls scheme are currently being written.

    • Date: January 2015
    • Focus: Design, Manufacturing, Controls, Physics
